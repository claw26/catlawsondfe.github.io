Skills Bootcamps offer free, flexible courses of up to 16 weeks. They help you to build up specialist skills. At the end of the course, you’ll get an interview with a local employer.

You are eligible to apply for a Skills Bootcamp if you are:

  • aged 19 or over
  • employed, self-employed, have recently become unemployed or are returning to work after a break
  • serving a prison sentence and due to be released within 6 months of completing a Skills Bootcamp, or on temporary release

Some Skills Bootcamps may have additional eligibility criteria.

Contact the Skills Bootcamp provider to find out if you’re eligible and how to apply.

HGV driving: new to HGV driving

These courses will help you become a qualified heavy goods vehicle (HGV) driver and get the Driver Certificate of Professional Competence (CPC).

If you’ve got a full car licence or medium-sized vehicle licence (category C1), these Skills Bootcamps will help you qualify to drive either:

  • a rigid HGV (category C) - these are used for lots of commercial deliveries throughout the UK, some have loading access at the rear and sides as well as electronic tail lifts which makes them easier to load and unload
  • an articulated lorry (category C+E) - these have 2 parts (the cab and the trailer the cab is pulling) and are mainly used in long-distance haulage or for the movement of large volumes of items as well as bulky or heavy goods

Check with the course provider to see if they have any extra criteria for applying.

HGV driving: upgrade your current HGV licence

If you’ve currently got at least a rigid HGV (category C) licence, these Skills Bootcamps will help you upgrade to drive an articulated lorry (category C+E). You can also:

  • qualify to transport hazardous goods such as explosives, gases or flammable liquids
  • train to get your Petroleum Driver Passport so you can drive fuel tankers

Check with the course provider to see if they have any extra criteria for applying.

HGV driving: return to HGV driving

If you’re not currently working as a driver but have a rigid HGV (category C) licence or articulated lorry (category C+E) licence, these Skills Bootcamps will help you:

  • bring your Driver CPC qualification up to date so you can get back to driving
  • upgrade your rigid HGV licence so you can drive an articulated lorry

Vehicle categories have changed over time. You might know a category C licence as HGV class 2, and category C+E as HGV class 1.

Check with the course provider to see if they have any extra criteria for applying.
